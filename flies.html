Flies & Patterns

Flies are artificial lures used in fly fishing, designed to mimic the appearance and movement of aquatic insects, small fish, or other creatures that fish feed on. These flies are made from various materials, including feathers, fur, and synthetic fibers. There are two main types of flies: dry flies and wet flies.

  • Dry Flies: These float on the waterâ€™s surface and are designed to mimic adult insects, such as mayflies or caddisflies. Dry fly fishing is often considered the most challenging and rewarding form of fly fishing because it requires delicate casting and perfect timing to entice a fish to rise to the surface and take the fly.
  • Wet Flies: Unlike dry flies, wet flies are designed to sink below the surface, imitating nymphs or other aquatic life forms. Wet fly fishing can be done at different depths, depending on where fish are feeding in the water column.

    • In addition to dry and wet flies, there are also other types of flies like streamers (designed to resemble small fish) and nymphs (which imitate juvenile insects). Different patterns are used to match the local food sources of the fish you're targeting, making fly selection a critical part of the process.
