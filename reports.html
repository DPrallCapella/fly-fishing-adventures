Fishing Reports in Colorado

Stay up-to-date with the latest fishing reports to ensure the best experience on Colorado's beautiful rivers and lakes. Whether you're targeting trout, bass, or other species, having access to current conditions such as water temperature, flow rates, and recent catches is crucial for success. Below you'll find some of the best resources for fishing reports in Colorado:

1. Colorado Parks and Wildlife (CPW) Fishing Reports

Colorado Parks and Wildlife provides comprehensive and regularly updated fishing reports for various lakes, rivers, and reservoirs throughout the state. They cover fishing conditions, popular fishing spots, and species-specific insights:

  • CPW Fishing Conditions - Find fishing conditions by region, including reports on water clarity, temperature, and recent fish activity.
  • CPW Fish Species and Fishing Locations - Explore detailed information about different species and the best places to catch them in Colorado.

2. Local Fishing Forums and Community Reports

Local fishing forums are an excellent source of real-time fishing reports, as anglers from around the state share their experiences and tips. Check out the following forums for insider information:

  • Colorado Fisherman Forum - A community forum where anglers share their fishing reports, techniques, and recent catches across Colorado.
  • Fly Fisherman Forums - A popular online community for fly fishing enthusiasts, with specific sections dedicated to Colorado fishing conditions.

3. Local Fly Shops and Outfitters

Many local fly shops and outfitters post daily or weekly fishing reports based on the rivers and lakes they guide on. These reports often include information on what flies are working best, which techniques are effective, and any changes in fishing conditions:

  • Angler's Covey Fishing Reports - One of the top fly shops in Colorado Springs, providing detailed fishing reports for local waters such as the South Platte River.
  • Trouts Fly Fishing Reports - A Denver-based shop with excellent reports on conditions across Colorado, including hatch charts and fly recommendations.
  • Fish Colorado - A popular fishing outfitter with up-to-date fishing reports and tips for Colorado’s best fishing locations.

4. Seasonal Fishing Insights

The fishing conditions in Colorado can vary greatly depending on the time of year. Here are some tips for different seasons:

Spring (March – May)

Spring is a great time for trout fishing as they become more active after winter. Keep an eye on water temperatures, as snowmelt can cause fluctuations in flow rates. Popular spots include the South Platte River and Colorado River.

Summer (June – August)

During the summer months, fishing is great early in the morning or later in the evening to avoid hot midday temperatures. Many anglers target trout, bass, and pike in mountain lakes and rivers, such as the Arkansas River and Cherry Creek Reservoir.

Fall (September – November)

Fall brings cooler temperatures, which can result in prime fishing conditions, especially for trout. The fall spawn also makes this a good time for targeting larger fish. The Gunnison River and Roaring Fork River are top destinations.

Winter (December – February)

Winter fishing in Colorado can be peaceful and productive, especially in areas like Pueblo Reservoir. Ice fishing becomes popular on many lakes and reservoirs across the state, and it's a good time for trout, walleye, and northern pike.

5. Colorado River Flow & Water Conditions

Understanding the flow rates and water levels in Colorado's rivers can make or break your fishing trip. Several websites provide real-time flow and water data:

  • USGS Colorado Water Science Center - Provides current river flow data for many of Colorado's rivers and streams, including the Colorado River and South Platte River.
  • Colorado Division of Water Resources - Offers detailed real-time data on stream flows, lake levels, and reservoir conditions throughout the state.

For the best fishing experience, always check the current fishing reports and conditions before heading out. Colorado offers some of the best fly fishing in the country, and staying informed will help you catch the biggest fish!
